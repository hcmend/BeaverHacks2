What is PEMDAS?

PEMDAS is an acronym that helps you remember the order of operations in mathematics:

  • P: Parentheses
  • E: Exponents
  • M: Multiplication
  • D: Division
  • A: Addition
  • S: Subtraction

When solving math problems, always start with operations inside parentheses, then move to exponents, followed by multiplication and division (from left to right), and finally addition and subtraction (from left to right).

Example Problems

Here are some examples to help you understand PEMDAS:

  • Example 1: (2 + 3) × 4 = 20

    Step 1: Solve inside the parentheses: (2 + 3) = 5

    Step 2: Multiply: 5 × 4 = 20

    Solution: 20

  • Example 2: 5 + 23 = 13

    Step 1: Solve the exponent: 23 = 8

    Step 2: Add: 5 + 8 = 13

    Solution: 13

  • Example 3: 10 ÷ (2 × 5) = 1

    Step 1: Solve inside the parentheses: (2 × 5) = 10

    Step 2: Divide: 10 ÷ 10 = 1

    Solution: 1
